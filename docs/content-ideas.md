# blocmates. — Content Ideas Bank

This file is the reference bank for **variable segment ideas**. Every show has 2 rotating variable segments (positions 4 and 5 in the show order). Pull from this list when building a new episode, or use it as a starting point for web searches to find the most current angle on each topic.

Brody will paste raw ideas directly into this file. Clean them up, group them, and select the 2 most timely/relevant for the current episode based on what's happening that week.

---

## How to Use This File

1. Read the segment ideas below
2. Cross-reference with the week's news (do a web search) to find the most timely angle
3. Pick 2 segments that complement the fixed segments — High Signal covers news, The Tool Shed covers tools, so variable segments should add depth, debate, or a fresh format
4. Build 2–4 slides per variable segment following the slide rules in `show-style-guide.md`

---

## ⚠️ Note on Fixed Segment Overlaps

A few ideas below overlap with fixed segments. Don't use these as variable segments — they're already baked into the show structure:

- **The Rundown** → This is essentially what **High Signal** already does. Could be used as an alternate name/format for that segment if the show evolves.
- **Tool Showcase / Prompt Lab / The Automation Playbook** → These overlap heavily with **The Tool Shed**. Pull from these when building The Tool Shed content, not as variable slots.

## Segment Ideas

---

### Live Demos & Hands-On

High-engagement formats where the host actually does something on screen in real time.

**Vibe-Code Corner**
Live build session using AI coding tools (Cursor, Claude, Replit, etc.) — building a bot, automating a workflow, or prototyping an app. The audience watches it happen, mistakes and all.
*Best paired with:* a current news story about a tool or capability to make it timely.

**This Week I Tried…**
Personal segment where the host shares an AI tool, workflow, or experiment they actually used in their own life or work that week. Genuine, relatable, and sometimes funny when things don't work.
*Best paired with:* whatever was genuinely useful (or a disaster) that week — great for authentic moments.

---

### Market & Investing

**Alpha Drop**
The investing and trading angle. Where is smart money flowing in AI? Sectors, companies, or tokens worth watching. Macro narrative analysis, not just price talk.
*Best paired with:* a week with notable funding news, token moves, or sector rotation stories in High Signal.

---

### Analysis & Debate

High-discussion formats that generate audience engagement and positions the show as opinionated.

**AI vs. Reality**
Take a hyped AI claim, product, or narrative and stress-test it. Does this actually work? Is this a real use case or just a demo? Builds trust by being willing to call BS when warranted.
*Best paired with:* any week with an overhyped product launch or viral AI claim in the news.

**Hot Takes**
Rapid-fire opinions on trending AI topics. Host gives their unfiltered take, audience can agree or fight about it in the community. Intentionally provocative and discussion-generating.
*Best paired with:* any week where there's genuine controversy or strong opinion in the space.

**Rabbit Hole**
Deep-dive into one specific AI topic the host is genuinely curious about. Could be technical (how does a transformer work?), philosophical (will AI replace creative jobs?), or niche (AI in agriculture). Curiosity-led, not news-led.
*Best paired with:* a slower news week where a deep dive adds more value than more surface-level coverage.

---

### People & Community

**Guest Spotlight**
Casual conversation with a builder, founder, researcher, or trader. Not a formal interview — more of a "what are you seeing right now" vibe. The guest's current focus is the segment.
*Best paired with:* any week a relevant person is available or there's a story connected to their work.

**Build in Public**
Recurring series tracking a real project from idea to launch using AI tools. Could be a personal project or a community request. Audience follows along week to week and sees the honest process.
*Best used as:* a multi-week arc, not a one-off. Great for audience retention and accountability.

**Community Roast**
Audience submits their AI projects, apps, or workflows and the host reviews them live. Honest, constructive feedback with some humor. Drives engagement and makes the audience feel seen.
*Best paired with:* after a call-to-action in a previous episode asking for submissions.

**Collab Episode**
Joint episode with another creator, podcast, or community. Cross-pollinates audiences and brings fresh energy. Could also be a structured debate on a hot topic.
*Best used as:* a special or milestone episode format rather than a regular weekly slot.

---

### Big Picture & Industry

**Industry Watch**
How is AI transforming a specific industry this week? Healthcare, finance, manufacturing, creative, legal — real examples of actual AI deployment, not research papers. Keeps the content grounded in the real world.
*Best paired with:* a week where High Signal has strong stories in a specific vertical.

**State of AI**
Monthly or quarterly solo segment that zooms out and gives the host's big-picture view on where AI is heading, what's changed, and what the audience should be paying attention to. Positions the host as a macro thinker.
*Best used as:* a once-a-month format, not every week. High-effort, high-signal.

---

### The Tool Shed Supplement

These ideas overlap with the fixed The Tool Shed segment. Use them when building content for that segment, not as variable slots.

**Tool Showcase**
Live demo of an AI tool the audience can use right now. The host actually uses it on screen, shows what it does, and gives an honest take. If it's bad and they're not a sponsor, say so.

**Prompt Lab**
Share and workshop specific prompts, workflows, or agent configurations that produce real results. Tactical, immediately actionable — the audience can copy these and use them today.

**The Automation Playbook**
Step-by-step walkthrough of automating a real task using AI (email sorting, research pipelines, data entry, social media scheduling). Practical and massively valuable.

**AI Fails & Lessons**
Showcase entertaining or instructive AI failures — from personal experience or the community. What went wrong, why, and what it teaches. Humanizes AI and keeps the vibe honest. Can be woven into any segment.

## Notes for Claude

When selecting variable segments:
- Avoid topics already covered in High Signal (that segment handles top news)
- Avoid Tool Showcase, Prompt Lab, and Automation Playbook as variable slots — those feed into The Tool Shed
- Prefer segments that generate discussion or debate over pure information delivery
- Alpha Drop works best when there's a clear market narrative that week — don't force it
- Always do a current web search to validate the topic is still relevant and find fresh angles
- Build in Public and Collab Episode are multi-week or special formats — flag for Brody before including them
