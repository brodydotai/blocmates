🔴 LEAD — Oracle's $108B debt crisis layoffs
Oracle planning up to 30,000 layoffs (12-18% of workforce) as AI data center spending creates a cash crisis. Total debt hit $108.1B after an $18B bond issuance. Analysts warn of asset sales (Cerner) to stay afloat. Private credit market showing cracks — banks pulling back from Oracle-linked data center lending. Somebody built a job loss dashboard to track the damage in real time.
links
Microsoft launches Copilot Cowork release
Satya Nadella announced Copilot Cowork — hand off a task and it builds a plan, then executes across M365 apps within enterprise security/governance. Powered by Anthropic's Claude Sonnet models. $30/user/month. Research preview now, broader rollout late March. This is basically corporate-compliant Cowork — the enterprise wrapper around agentic AI.
link
Perplexity Personal Computer release
Perplexity dropped two products at their first dev conference. "Perplexity Computer" runs in the cloud as a general-purpose digital worker. "Personal Computer" runs locally on a Mac mini with full file/app access. Positioned as a safer OpenClaw alternative — all actions confirmed by user, built-in audit trail. The agentic race just got another serious contender.
link
AI goes political — data center prohibition politics
Progressives on Capitol Hill gaining traction on a federal data center moratorium. Sanders preparing legislation. Georgia, Vermont, Virginia, New York all considering construction freezes. Rising electricity costs from AI boom becoming a midterm election wedge issue. This is AI leaving the tech bubble and entering mainstream politics.

⠿ 03 the tool shed
~10 min
Cloudflare crawl API tool
Cloudflare launched a /crawl endpoint in open beta — one API call crawls an entire website. Give it a URL and it follows links, sitemaps, and pages up to configurable depth. Returns HTML, Markdown, or JSON. Crawl jobs run async for up to 7 days, results stored 14 days. Free and paid Workers plans. Respects robots.txt. This is the simplest web scraping tool that's ever existed.
link
Anthropic skills guide resource
Anthropic released the complete 32-page guide to building skills for Claude. Skills are folders of instructions, scripts, and resources that Claude loads dynamically to improve performance on specialized tasks. Upload via Settings > Capabilities > Skills. Official repo at github.com/anthropics/skills has production examples. This is the playbook for making Claude actually useful for your specific workflows.

⠇ 04 distributed compute
~15 min
The zeitgeist: decentralized AI overview
The theme this week is unmistakable: AI is decentralizing. Perplexity is running inference on Mac minis. Politicians are trying to freeze data center construction. And open networks are training 70B parameter models without anyone's permission. The centralized compute monopoly is being challenged from every angle — hardware, policy, and protocol.
Templar: permissionless 70B training project
Templar is a decentralized training network on Bittensor. Anyone with a GPU can contribute to training frontier models — no permission required. TEMPLAR III is their live 70B parameter run using SparseLoCo, which communicates only 1-3% of gradients with 2-bit quantization. It outperforms DiLoCo and DeMo on communication efficiency. This is real distributed training happening right now, not a whitepaper.
link
Hermes Agent: open source agent that grows project
Nous Research launched Hermes Agent — an open source personal agent with persistent multi-level memory that gets smarter over time. It has dedicated machine access and remembers what it learns across sessions. Hermes 4.3 is the first model trained fully decentralized over the internet using Psyche. This is the convergence: decentralized training producing decentralized agents.
link
Venice.ai: private AI goes onchain project
Venice.ai is Erik Voorhees' private, uncensored AI platform — built on open-source models with decentralized inference. No data stored, no conversation logs. The VVV token on Base lets users stake for permanent API capacity: stake 1% of supply, get 1% of throughput forever. It's the crypto-native answer to centralized AI — inference that nobody can shut down, censor, or surveil.
link
⠿ 05 money talks
~10 min
LeCun's $1B bet against LLMs funding
Yann LeCun left Meta and raised $1.03B for AMI Labs at $3.5B pre-money — Europe's largest-ever seed deal. Building "world models" based on JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture). The thesis: LLMs are dead, AI needs to learn from reality, not language. Backed by Bezos Expeditions, Mark Cuban, Nvidia. HQ in Paris with offices in NYC, Montreal, Singapore. Four months from founding to billion-dollar raise.
link
Murati + Nvidia + Replit: follow the cash deals
Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab locked in a gigawatt-scale Nvidia deal — deploying Vera Rubin GPUs for frontier model training, targeted 2027. Nvidia made a "significant investment" on top. Meanwhile Replit tripled its valuation to $9B with a $400M Series D in 6 months. 150K paying customers, $240M revenue in 2025, targeting $1B run-rate by end of 2026. Georgian Partners led. Shaq and Jared Leto are investors.
